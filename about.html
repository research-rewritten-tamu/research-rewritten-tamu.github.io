What is Research, Re-written?

If I look back to my first year of graduate school, the one skill I felt the most unprepared to use was the ability to read and digest scientific papers. Unlike coding or solving equations, I had no formal training in understanding research papers, and faced a steep learning curve.

One activity that really helped me develop this paper-reading skill was journal club, where members of the astronomy group at A&M would present quick recaps of recent papers on a weekly basis. Watching older grad students and postdocs talk about the key findings of papers gave me a better idea of how to extract the highlights from papers and clearly communicate these results to others.

However, the problem that I have with journal club is that I forget about the papers that were discussed very quickly, and feel as if a more hands-on approach to reading papers would help them stick a little better. As such, I had an idea to start Research, Re-written: a program that would group up grad students and undergrads, and have them write astrobite-level articles together.
